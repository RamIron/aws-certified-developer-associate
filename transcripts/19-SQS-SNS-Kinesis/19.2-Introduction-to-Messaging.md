# 19.2 - Introduction to Messaging

## Key Concepts

### 1. Two Patterns of Application Communication

| Pattern | How it works | Coupling |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Synchronous** | App A talks directly to App B | Tight — they depend on each other |
| **Asynchronous / Event-based** | Apps communicate via a middleware (queue) | Loose — they never talk directly |

### 2. The Problem with Synchronous Communication
- Works fine under normal load
- **Breaks under sudden traffic spikes** — one service overwhelms the other
- Example: normally encode 10 videos/day → spike to 1,000 → encoding service crashes

### 3. The Solution: Decouple with Middleware
- Insert a **queue or messaging layer** between services
- The middleware absorbs the spike and scales independently
- Producer just drops messages in; consumer picks them up at its own pace

### 4. The Three AWS Decoupling Services

| Service | Model | Use Case |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **SQS** | Queue | Decouple producers and consumers asynchronously |
| **SNS** | Pub/Sub | Push messages to multiple subscribers at once |
| **Kinesis** | Real-time streaming | Big data ingestion and real-time processing |

## Exam Tips
- **Synchronous** = direct connection = risky under spikes
- **Asynchronous** = queue/middleware in between = resilient and scalable
- SQS = queue · SNS = pub/sub · Kinesis = real-time streaming
- All three scale independently — services using them can scale independently too
