# 9.19 - 3rd Party Domains & Route 53

## Key Concepts

### 1. Domain Registrar vs DNS Service

- **Domain Registrar** — where you **buy** your domain name (pay annual charges)
  - Examples: Amazon Registrar, GoDaddy, Google Domains
- **DNS Service** — where you **manage** your DNS records
  - Example: Route 53 hosted zones
- They are **different things**, even though registrars usually include a DNS service

### 2. Using Route 53 with a 3rd Party Domain

You can buy a domain on GoDaddy but use Route 53 to manage DNS:

```
Step 1: Buy domain on GoDaddy (example.com)

Step 2: Create a Public Hosted Zone in Route 53 for example.com

Step 3: Copy the 4 NS (Name Server) records from Route 53

Step 4: Update the Name Servers on GoDaddy to point to Route 53's NS records

Result: DNS queries → GoDaddy says "ask Route 53" → Route 53 manages all records
```

---

## Exam Tips
- Domain Registrar ≠ DNS Service (different concepts)
- You can use **any registrar** + **Route 53 as DNS** by updating the NS records
- To use Route 53 with a 3rd party domain: create public hosted zone → update NS records on the registrar
